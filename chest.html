Muscle Information

There are three primary muscles involved in moving the chest. The major pectoralis, the minor pectoralis and the serratus anterior. They are primarily associated with the movement of shoulders and amrs.

Pectoralis Major

The pectoralis major, also known as the "pec" is most critical part of the pectoral region. It is located just over the anterior ribcage on both sides of the chest. It is overall involved with movement of the shoulders and arms. It is responsible for the movement and rotation of the arm. It is used in heavy activities such as lifting, throwing, pushing etc.

Pectoralis Major

As the name suggest it is a smaller muscle than pectoralis major. It is a muscle shaped as a triagle that is below the pectoralis major and connects through the 3rd, 4th and 5th ribs to the scapula. It is used to control the inward movement of the shoulders. It is used when the shoulders are rolled inward.

Serratus Anterior

It is below the pectoralis major and is used to pull the scapula forward around the thorax.

Bench Press

Lay flat on the bench with your feet touching the ground

Roll your shoulders ourwards and create an arched gap between your back and bench

Bring the bar to your mid chest.

Raise the bar until you have extended your elbows

Incline Dumbell Press

Raise the bench in a 30 degree angle

Lay on the bench and put your feet on the ground

Bring them slowly down until about shoulder width

Raise the dumbells

Dumbell Flys

Lay on a flat bench with your feet on the ground

Hold the dumbells above chest and have your elbows slightly bent

Simultaneously lower the weights to either sid

Stop when the weights are in line with the bench

Raise your hands to the starting position
